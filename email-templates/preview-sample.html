No.01 — The Letter
On the days I don't want to.

I trained yesterday at 5:47 AM and I didn't want to. Not in a dramatic way — just the regular kind of not-wanting where the bed is warm, the sky is dark, and the squat rack is forty minutes away.

I went anyway. Not because I'm disciplined or because I love it every day, but because four years ago I made a promise to myself that I wouldn't bargain with the work anymore. The lifts I do at 5:47 AM aren't for me at 5:47 AM. They're for me on stage, six months from now, in a pose I haven't earned yet.

If you're in your own version of the dark, cold, forty-minutes-away — go. The version of you you're building doesn't care how you felt when you started.

No.02 — This Month's Focus
The Hip Hinge — Master This First.

If your glutes aren't growing, 9 times out of 10 it's the hinge. Most people squat with their lower back and call it a glute exercise. The hinge is the foundation that fixes everything else — RDLs, deadlifts, hip thrusts, all of it.

The Move
Romanian Deadlift

3 sets of 10. Soft knees, bar travels close to the body, hips push backwards (not down). You should feel a deep stretch through the hamstrings — that's the hinge working.

"Push your hips backwards, like you're closing a car door."
